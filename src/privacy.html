Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to LLM Router. We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle data when you use our proxy service.

2. Data Collection & Usage

LLM Router is designed as a lightweight, privacy-first pass-through proxy. We collect minimal information:

  • API Keys: API keys for third-party providers are stored locally on your server or device. We do not transmit these keys to any central server owned by LLM Router.
  • Prompts and Responses: Your prompts and the responses generated by LLMs pass directly through your local instance of LLM Router to the respective third-party providers. We do not persistently store, log, or read the content of your requests.
  • Ephemeral Logs: A short-term, in-memory log of recent requests (including timestamps, provider used, and token counts) is kept for dashboard analytics. This data is cleared when the application restarts.

3. Third-Party Providers

When you use LLM Router, your data is sent to the third-party API providers you have configured (e.g., Groq, Cerebras, OpenRouter). Each provider has its own privacy policy and data retention practices.

We strongly recommend reviewing the privacy policies of the providers you actively use, particularly regarding whether they use your prompts for model training.

4. Analytics and Telemetry

We do not collect remote telemetry, tracking data, or usage metrics from your self-hosted instances.

5. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Since the software is primarily self-hosted, updates will be reflected in new versions of the repository.

6. Contact

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please refer to the project's repository and open an issue.
